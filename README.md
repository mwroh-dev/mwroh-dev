### 🙋‍♂️ About Me

I am a **Full-stack Engineer with a frontend background** and around 8 years of experience building web products.

I started with frontend development and gradually expanded into full-stack and backend work with **React**, **TypeScript**, **Node.js**, and **NestJS**. Over the past few years, I have worked on systems with a lot of domain rules, including **medical EMR**, **veterinary EMR**, **insurance claim workflows**, and **clinical laboratory device integrations**.

That work taught me to look past screens and APIs and ask how the real workflow behaves: what the inputs are, where state changes, where handoffs happen, and how results are checked.

Recently, I have been exploring how **AI agent workflows** might fit into practical work. I am still building and learning in this area through public repositories, writing, and small workflow tools.

### 🔎 What I'm Exploring

I am interested in turning repeated work into smaller steps that AI workflows can handle.

One current thread is a marketing-related workflow tool. I have been using it as a practical case for taking a person's context, criteria, references, and review process, then shaping them into inputs an AI-assisted workflow can use.

Some questions I keep coming back to:

- **How can vague requests become usable inputs?**
- **What should be handled by code, by AI, or by a person?**
- **Can a prompt become part of a reusable workflow instead of a one-off request?**
- **What evidence is needed before saying the output is correct?**

### 📌 Engineering Background

  Built Cloud EMR features connected to clinical workflows such as treatment, lab tests, surgery, prescriptions, and billing.

- **Claims and domain rules**  
  Worked with medical domain rules and external claim specifications, implementing them as service features and file-generation logic.

- **Device integration and backend work**  
  Worked on clinical laboratory device integrations, handling TCP, Serial, and file-based data flows and connecting test results back into EMR workflows.

### 🧩 What Drives Me

I care about finding structure inside messy work.

Before building, I tend to look for the inputs, state changes, exceptions, review points, and verification criteria behind the task.

In the past, that led me to EMR systems, claim workflows, and device integrations. Now, the same interest is leading me toward **AI agent workflows**, **prompt structure**, **verification**, and **reusable automation**.
